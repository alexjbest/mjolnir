Definition1.2.15Induced (pre)sheaves
Given a (pre)sheaf \(\F\) on a space \(X\) and a continuous map \(f\colon X \to Y\) we have an induced (pre)sheaf, denoted \(f_*\F\) on \(Y\) defined by \[(f_*\F)(U) = \F(f^{-1}(U))\] for \(U\) open in \(Y\). With the restriction maps coming from \(\F\) as \(V \subset U\) implies \(f^{-1}(V)\subset f^{-1}(U)\). It should be checked that indeed \(\F\) being a (pre)sheaf implies \(f_*\F\) is a (pre)sheaf.
