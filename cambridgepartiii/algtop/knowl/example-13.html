Example1.4.20Chains generating \(H_n(S^n, *)\)
  1. Choose \(f\colon \Delta^n \to \Delta^n\) a homeomorphism and let \(e\colon \Delta^n \to \Delta^n\) be the identity map. Then \(a_{n-1} = f_\#(de) \in C_*(S^{n-1})\) and we have \(d a_{n-1} = df_\#(de) = f_\# (d^2 e) = 0\) and so \(a_{n-1}\) is closed.
  2. Choose \(g\colon (D^n/S^{n-1}, S^{n-1}/S^{n-1}) \to (S^n, *)\). Now let \(b_n = g_\#(f_\#(e)) \in C_n(S^n,*)\). Then \(db_n = g_\#(a_{n-1}) \in C_*(*)\) implies \(b_n\) is closed in \(C_*(S^n, *)\).
    For example if \(n = 2\) we are crushing the boundary of the \(2\)-simplex to a point.
