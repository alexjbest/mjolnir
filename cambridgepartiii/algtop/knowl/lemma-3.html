Lemma1.3.7
\(d^2 = 0\)
Proof
It suffices to show \(d^2(e_I) = 0\) for all \(I\). \begin{align*} d^2(e_I) &= d\left(\sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-1)^j e_{i_0,\ldots,\hat{i}_j,\ldots,i_k}\right)\\ &= \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-1)^j d\left(e_{i_0,\ldots,\hat{i}_j,\ldots,i_k} \right)\\ &= \sum_{j = 0}^{k} (-1)^j\left( \sum_{l \lt j} (-1)^l e_{i_0,\ldots,\hat{i}_l,\ldots,\hat{i}_j,\ldots,i_k} + \sum_{l \gt j} (-1)^{l-1} e_{i_0,\ldots,\hat{i}_j,\ldots,\hat{i}_l,\ldots,i_k}\right)\\ &= \sum_{j = 0}^{k} \left( \sum_{l \lt j} (-1)^{l+j} e_{I - i_l - i_j} - \sum_{l \gt j} (-1)^{l+j} e_{I - i_j - i_l}\right) = 0. \end{align*}
