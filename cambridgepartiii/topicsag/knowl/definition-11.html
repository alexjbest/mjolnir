Definition1.3.8Projective morphisms
If \(Y\) is any scheme \(\PP_{\ZZ}^n = \Proj \ZZ[x_0,\ldots,x_n]\) thne \(\PP_Y^n = Y \times_{\Spec \ZZ}\PP_{\ZZ}^n\) is projective space over \(Y\). A morphism \(f\colon X \to Y \) is projective if it factors as \(X \xrightarrow{i} \PP_Y^n \to Y\) with \(i\) a closed immersion and \(\PP_Y^n \to Y\) the projection.
