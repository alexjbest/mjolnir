Definition1.2.12Morphisms of locally ringed spaces
A morphism \((X,\sO_x) \to (Y,\sO_Y)\) of locally ringed spaces is a continuous map \(f\colon X \to Y\) along with a morphism of sheaves of rings \[ f^\# \colon \sO_Y \to \sO_X \] such that the induced maps \(f_p^\# \colon \sO_{Y,f(p)} \to \sO_{X,p}\) are local homomorphisms for all \(p\). The maps \(f_p^\#\) are induced by \((U,s) \mapsto (U,f^\#(s))\).
