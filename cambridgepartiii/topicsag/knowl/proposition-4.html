Proposition1.3.6
\(X\) is locally Noetherian is and only if for every affine open subset \(\Spec A \subset X\) we have \(A\) Noetherian. In particular \(\Spec A\) is noetherian if and only if \(A\) is Noetherian.
Proof
\(\Leftarrow\) clear, \(\Rightarrow\) let \(U \subset X\) be open affine, \(U = \Spec A\). In general if \(B\) is Notherian and \(f \in B\) then \(B_f\) is Noetherian and \(D(f) \cong \Spec B_p\) as schemes. Also the \(D(f)\)s form a basis for the topology on \(\Spec B\). Thus any open set of \(\Spec B\) can be covered by open affines of the form \(\Spec B_f \) with \(B_f\) Noetherian. \(U \cap \Spec B\) can be covered by affine schemes of the form \(\Spec B_f\) with \(B_f \) Noetherian. We need to show that if \(\Spec A\) can be covered by sets of the form \(\Spec B\) with \(B\) Noetherian, then \(A\) is Noetherian.
