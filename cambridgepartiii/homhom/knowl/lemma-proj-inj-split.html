Lemma1.6.3
If \(C\) is projective or \(A\) is injective then \[ 0 \to A \to B \to C \to 0 \] is split.
Proof
(We prove the \(C\) projective case) Consider \[ \xymatrix{ & C \ar@{=}[d]_f \ar[dl]^s&\\ B \ar[r]^q &C \ar[r] & 0} \] then \(gs = 1\). Now produce \(r\) such that \(rf = 1_A\), \(fr + sg = 1_B\) and \(rs = 0\). Let \(h = 1 -sg\). Now \(gh = 0\) giving that \(h = fr\) by the properties of the kernel. \[ \xymatrix{B \ar[rr]^h\ar[rd]^{\exists ! r} &&B \ar[r]^g &C \\ & A \ar[ur] &} \] Now check \(rf = 1_A\) and \(rs = 0\).
