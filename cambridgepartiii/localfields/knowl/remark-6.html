Remark3.1.10
Uniqueness in Hensel's . We saw that every element of \(K^*\) is of the form \( u \pi^r\) for some \(u \in R^*\) and \(m \in \ZZ\). If \(R= \cO_v\) for some valuation \(v \colon K^* \to \RR\) then \(v(x) \ge 0\) for all \(x \in R\). So if \(u \in R^*\) then \(v(u) = 0\). In particular \(v(u\pi^m) = m v(\pi)\), i.e. \(v\) is uniquely determined by \(v(\pi)\) so it is unique if we normalise it.
