Theorem3.3.4
Let \(K\) be field complete w.r.t. a discrete valuation \(v\). If \(\chara K \gt 0\) and \(k\) is finite then \(K \cong k((t))\).
Proof
\(\chara(K) = \chara(k) = p\) and \(|k| = q = p^l\). Let \(\alpha,\beta \in k\) so we have \(([\alpha] + [\beta])^q = [\alpha]^q + [\beta]^q = [\alpha] + [\beta]\) and hence \([\alpha + \beta] = [\alpha] + [\beta]\). Therefore the Teichmuller map \(k \hookrightarrow K\) is a field embedding. By lemma 2.3 \[K = \left\{ \sum_{n \ge n_0}^\infty a_n \pi^n : a_n \in k\right\}\xrightarrow{\sim} k((t))\] via the map \(\pi \mapsto t\).
