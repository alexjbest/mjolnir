Definition3.3.1Teichmuller representatives
Let \(k\) be complete w.r.t. a discrete valuation \(v\). Suppose that the residue field \(k\) is finite, say \(|k| = q\). Let \(f(x) = x^q - x \in \cO[X]\). Each \(\alpha \in k\) is a simple root of \(\bar f \in k[x]\). Hensel's lemma implies that there is a unique \(a \in\cO\) s.t. \(a^q = a\) and \(a \equiv \alpha \pmod{\pi}\). The \(a \in \cO\) constructed here is the Teichmuller representative for \(\alpha \in k\).
